Capital raising

Phase 1

  1. We create comprehensive business plans outlining avenues for growth, investment needs, structure and timelines of the deal.
  2. We prepare detailed investment collateral, including presentations and memos, to inform and attract potential investors.

Phase 2

  1. We meticulously select and engage with potential investors, manage presentations, negotiations, and analyze proposals.

Phase 3

  1. We are actively involved in securing the best possible investment terms, creating sound legal and tax frameworks and driving client’s team through the due diligence process.
  2. We coordinate with legal advisors to ensure meticulous documentation and execution for successful deal closure.

Marat Fattakhov

Marat is the founder and a managing partner at LM Investments, with an 18-year tenure in strategic consulting, M&A, capital raising, and implementing investment projects ranging from startups to national-scale initiatives. He specializes in M&A transactions within the IT sector, innovative and high-tech manufacturing, and the oil and gas industry.

Marat is the winner of the International Global CIO "Project of the Year 2018", among other competitions and awards.

A Ph.D. in Economics, Marat is also a member of the International Union of Economists, holding a general consultative status with the United Nations Economic and Social Council.

Levon Ayrapetyan

Levon is the founder and partner in LM Investments with over 10 years of experience in M&A and corporate finance. As a Deloitte alumni Levon has and extensive experience in managing large international deals. He has participated in the closure of investment deals worth more than $8 billion (private equity, venture capital).

Levon's expertise lies in transactions in the agro-industrial complex, machinery manufacturing, forestry, transportation, and storage sectors. In collaboration with major investors, corporates, and business associations, he actively promotes investment activity in these industries.

Levon holds a Ph.D. in Economics.
